# John's Pro Roofing — Ground-Up SEO Content Strategy

A single document that takes the new website from blank to launch-ready, built to rank over time and convert from day one — plus a clear upsell roadmap for later phases.

Five parts:
1. **Scope & Expectations**
2. **Phase 1 Architecture** (what he paid for — build now)
3. **Phase 2+ Roadmap** (the upsell: locations → materials → blog → commercial)
4. **Research Plan** (free tools)
5. **The Writing Prompt** (produces all Phase 1 content in one shot)

---

# PART 1 — SCOPE & EXPECTATIONS

**Paid scope (Phase 1):** a focused, NJ-targeted site — Home, About, Services, Contact, FAQ, a Roofing hub with 5 roofing service pages, plus Siding and Decks. Roofing is primary; siding and decks are secondary (lighter, not aggressive). No location pages yet (Phase 2 upsell).

**Geography:** NJ-primary. Target NJ *through* the service pages (local language + schema), not through separate geo pages. **Mention** Pennsylvania once (Home/Contact/About + schema service-area); do **not** target it — no PA in title tags, no PA pages, no PA keyword targeting. PA service content only ever gets built if John holds a valid PA Home Improvement Contractor registration (legal, not SEO).

**Realistic timing:** website-only, no ads — this is a **compounding** asset. New domains take ~3–6 months to gain organic traction. So Phase 1 optimizes for two things at once:
- **Built to rank** — correct structure, intent-matched pages, local signals — compounds over months.
- **Converts immediately** — every page turns any visitor (referrals, direct, his existing Google Business Profile, word of mouth) into a call or quote.

**Build everything template-ready** so Phase 2 (locations especially) bolts on cheaply.

# PART 2 — PHASE 1 ARCHITECTURE (BUILD NOW)

Each page owns ONE primary search intent and ONE keyword cluster.

```
/                                   Home — brand + top services + trust + primary CTA
/about                              About — E-E-A-T: John's story, license, insurance, warranty
/services                           Services overview — links to all service pages
/roofing                            Roofing hub — overview + links to all roofing pages
  /commercial-roofing               Commercial — property-manager intent
    /commercial-roofing/repair      Commercial repair (nested child)
  /residential-roofing              Residential — homeowner intent
    /residential-roofing/repair     Residential repair (nested child)
  /emergency-roof-leak-repair       Urgent/emergency intent — top level, ranks fast
  /roof-inspections                 Low-pressure entry point
  /flat-roofing                     Material/system specialty
/siding                             Secondary service
/decks                              Secondary service
/faq                                8–12 homeowner questions (snippet-optimized)
/contact                            Phone, form, hours, service area
```

3 top-level services: **Roofing (primary), Siding, Decks.** Roofing gets **7 dedicated pages** (client-specified); siding and decks get one solid page each. That's **9 service pages** this round.

### The 7 roofing pages (client-specified, nested)
These are set by the client — do not substitute:
1. **Commercial Roofing** (parent) → **Commercial Roofing Repair** (child)
2. **Residential Roofing** (parent) → **Residential Roofing Repair** (child)
3. **Emergency Roof Leak Repair** — urgent intent, top level
4. **Roof Inspections**
5. **Flat Roofing**

Commercial and residential each split into an install/overview parent and a dedicated repair child. Emergency leak repair stays top-level (it's its own urgent intent and shouldn't be buried under one property type). The repair children target repair-intent keywords; the parents target install/replacement + general intent.

### Architecture rules
- **Local targeting lives in the service pages**, not geo pages: work his NJ county/region into copy, title tags, and LocalBusiness/RoofingContractor schema (e.g., "commercial roof repair in [region], NJ").
- **PA = one honest mention**, schema service-area only. No PA pages/targeting.
- **Build service pages as templates** — consistent structure so Phase 2 location variants ("[service] [town]") are fast to produce.
- **Avoid keyword cannibalization** — parent and child pages must target *different* intent (e.g., residential-roofing = "residential roof replacement"; residential-roofing/repair = "residential roof repair / leak"). Don't let the two compete for the same term.
- **Siding/Decks stay lighter** — solid single pages, not deep silos.

### Internal linking (build in now)
- Every service page → contact + financing CTA
- Each parent → its repair child (bidirectional)
- Emergency leak repair → both repair children + reviews/proof (visitor is stressed) → contact
- Roof inspections → all roofing pages (it's the entry funnel)
- Services hub → every service page; Roofing hub → all 7 roofing pages

# PART 3 — PHASE 2+ ROADMAP (THE UPSELL)

How mature roofing sites scale content — in ROI order. Each is a discrete, sellable phase, and Phase 1 is built so these bolt on.

**Phase 2 — Location pages (best ROI for a local roofer).**
Service × town: "roof repair [town]." The strongest local-SEO lever and the most natural upsell. Built right = service-page templates with localized copy, local proof, and town-specific schema. Built lazy (thin duplicates) = penalized — so this is done carefully, page by page, for towns John actually serves. *This is why Phase 1 service pages are built as templates.*

**Phase 3 — Material / roof-type pages (organic reach + authority).**
A page per type — asphalt shingle, metal, flat/TPO/EPDM (and later cedar/slate/tile). Captures comparison/research intent ("metal vs shingle cost," "how long does a flat roof last") the service pages miss, and builds topical authority that lifts the whole site. Lives under `/roof-types/`. Lower intent, cheap to produce, compounds.

**Phase 4 — Blog / resource layer (ongoing retainer content).**
Cost guides, comparisons, "signs you need a new roof," seasonal/storm prep. Highest-volume top-of-funnel content in roofing; each post links down into service + material + location pages. The easiest recurring upsell.

**Phase 5 — Commercial silo (only if he wants commercial work).**
A dedicated `/commercial-roofing/` track (different buyer, cycle, language; flat-roof systems, building types). Don't build speculatively — a single commercial mention inside the flat-roof page covers it until he commits.

**Natural growth path:** Phase 1 service pages → locations → materials → blog → commercial. Also a clean tiered-pricing story for proposals.

# PART 4 — RESEARCH PLAN (free tools, ~90 min)

Research serves the structure first, then feeds real terms into the prompt. In order:

### 4.1 Confirm facts from John (15 min — just ask him)
Full name; years in business; NJ HIC #; PA registration # (or NONE — decides PA scope); insurance carrier/status; manufacturer certs (GAF, Owens Corning, CertainTeed); exact warranty terms; confirmed NJ towns/region; whether solar (seen in reviews) is actually offered.

### 4.2 Keyword discovery (30 min — free, REAL data; biggest quality lever)
For each of the 5 roofing pages + siding/decks, gather actual queries:
- **Google autocomplete** (incognito): "roof repair [region]", "roof replacement cost", "flat roof coating", "storm damage roof", etc. — screenshot dropdowns.
- **People Also Ask + Related Searches** — become FAQ + future blog topics.
- **Google Keyword Planner** (free w/ any Google Ads account, no spend) — volume *ranges* + related terms, filtered to his NJ area. Best free demand signal.
- **AnswerThePublic / AlsoAsked** (limited free) — question-format queries.

Organize findings by the page each term belongs to. Paste into the prompt.

### 4.3 Competitor + gap audit (20 min)
Search his money keywords from his area; open top 3 organic + Maps 3-pack. Note: pages/services they have that he's missing, towns they target (Phase 2 fuel), blog topics, review counts (benchmark). Paste 1–3 competitor URLs into the prompt.

### 4.4 Local context (10 min)
NJ roofing permit basics for his counties; common local roof threats (nor'easters, wind, ice dams, hail) — authentic hooks for storm page + FAQ.

### 4.5 Asset inventory (5 min)
Authorized review quotes; before/after project photos; logo/brand assets.

## HOW THIS MAPS ONTO THE GREASTRO BUILD (implementation notes)

This strategy is the *content brief*. In this codebase, the architecture above is already expressed as content collections — write into those, do not invent new structures:

- **Roofing pages** = items in `src/content/roofing/` (`_meta.mdx` is the `/roofing` hub). Parent/child nesting uses the `parent:` frontmatter field (e.g. `commercial-roof-repair.mdx` has `parent: "commercial-roofing"`), not URL-segment folders. Keep existing slugs.
- **Top-level services** (Roofing/Siding/Decks) = `src/content/services/`. Roofing links to `/roofing`; siding/decks are their own pages.
- **FAQ** = `src/content/faq/` items, rendered via `AccordionVariant`. Write questions as homeowners search them; 40–60 word answers.
- **About** = `src/content/about-us/_meta.mdx` (E-E-A-T copy lives here + the about items).
- **Per-page SEO** = the `seo` frontmatter block (`metaTitle`, `metaDescription`, `keywords`) on each item / `_meta.mdx`. This is where meta titles/descriptions from the prompt go.
- **Business facts** = `src/content/siteData.ts` (name, address, phone, tagline). Service area = `src/content/service-areas/service-areas.json`.

- `service-areas.json` currently lists **NY and PA as equal targets** — strategy is NJ-primary, PA = soft mention only, no NY targeting.
- FAQ items still reference **"gutters" and "solar"** — gutters is dropped; solar is unconfirmed (`[NEEDS INPUT]`).
